Glove Talk

A wearable communication device designed to overcome speech difficulties by converting gestures into voice and text.

This is my favorite project so far – an idea I've nurtured for over 2 years. However, due to high development costs and lack of sponsorship, I have not been able to implement it.

To explain its origin: I am 24 years old and have a speech impairment (stutter and slurred speech). Besides my age, I have struggled to fully integrate into society, with only my family truly understanding me – which inspired this concept.

According to recent statistics, nearly 250,000 people in Kurdistan and Iraq suffer from speech impairments. Of these, about 40% are children and teenagers, and over 70% struggle to find employment or continue their education primarily due to communication challenges.

I aim to solve this issue with Glove Talk – a wearable device that converts hand gestures into speech and text, enabling direct communication.

The device functions as follows:

- A compact Raspberry Pi acts as the brain of the device.

- Custom flex sensors detect finger bending.

- An LCD screen and speaker display text and output sound.

- An ADS1115 module provides precise analog-to-digital conversion.

- An MPU 6050 sensor measures hand movement in all directions.

When the user interacts with the device, the sensors send the data to the Raspberry Pi where a dedicated program translates the gestures into words and sentences, which are then displayed and spoken.
